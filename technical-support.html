Frequently Asked Questions

What mobile phases are compatible with polysaccharide-based chiral columns?

CellaChrom® and CelluloSil® columns are compatible with hexane/isopropanol, hexane/ethanol (normal phase), and methanol/water, acetonitrile/water (reversed phase) systems. Avoid chlorinated solvents (DCM, chloroform) and THF in high concentrations as these can dissolve the coating. Always consult the column data sheet for specific solvent compatibility.

What mobile phase should I use with CrownSil® crown acid columns?

CrownSil® columns require an acidic aqueous mobile phase ??typically perchloric acid (HClO?? or trifluoroacetic acid (TFA) at pH 1??. These columns are operated exclusively in reversed-phase mode. Organic modifiers such as acetonitrile or methanol can be used to adjust retention.

How do I store chiral columns when not in use?

For polysaccharide-based columns (CellaChrom®, CelluloSil®): flush with pure isopropanol and store capped at room temperature away from UV light. For CrownSil®: flush with water/methanol (50:50) and store capped. Never let columns dry out or freeze.

Can CellaChrom columns be used with SFC systems?

Yes. CellaChrom® AD-3 and CelluloSil® OD-3 (3 µm particle size) are specifically recommended for SFC applications. Standard 5 µm columns can also be used for SFC with appropriate system back-pressure (??0 bar BPR). Contact us for SFC method development guidance.

What is the maximum operating pressure for CellaChrom columns?

CellaChrom® analytical columns are rated for a maximum operating pressure of 300 bar (4350 psi) for 5 µm columns and 400 bar (5800 psi) for 3 µm columns. Always operate within the specified pressure range to maintain column packing integrity.
